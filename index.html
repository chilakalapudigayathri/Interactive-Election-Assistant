Voter Registration

Before you can vote, you must be registered. Here's what you need to know.

  • 1Check if you are eligible (citizen, 18+).
  • 2Register online, by mail, or in person.
  • 3Verify your status before the deadline.
  • Eligibility requires citizenship, being at least 18, and meeting residency requirements in your jurisdiction.
  • Methods vary: online via government portals, mailing a printed form, or visiting local election offices in person.
  • Deadlines can be weeks before Election Day — register early to ensure no complications.
  • Always verify your status closer to election day to confirm your name is active on the electoral roll.

Voting Process

Step inside the polling station — here's what happens from arrival to casting your ballot.

  • 1Find your designated polling station.
  • 2Bring ID if required by your area.
  • 3Cast your ballot privately (paper or machine).
  • Locate your designated polling place ahead of time. You generally must vote at your assigned location.
  • Bring appropriate photo ID or other required documents per local voting laws.
  • Wait in line, verify your identity with poll workers, receive your ballot, and proceed to a private booth.
  • Fill the ballot carefully following instructions, review your choices, and submit it into the box or scanner.

Election Timeline

Elections don't happen overnight. Follow the journey from campaigning to certification.

  • 1Campaigning happens months before election.
  • 2Election Day is when everyone votes.
  • 3Results are counted and announced shortly after.
  • Campaign phase: candidates campaign for months or years to win party nominations and public support.
  • Early voting: many jurisdictions offer early or mail-in voting weeks before Election Day.
  • Election Day: the final official day for in-person voting. Polls open for specific hours.
  • Counting & certification: votes are tallied, verified, and officially certified over the following days or weeks.

From Campaign to Certification

A visual walkthrough of every major phase in the democratic process.

📣
Phase 1

Campaign & Primary

Candidates launch campaigns, debate, and compete in primaries or caucuses to secure their party's nomination. This can span months or even years.

📬
Phase 2

Voter Registration Closes

Registration deadlines fall weeks before Election Day. Ensure you're registered and your information is current in your jurisdiction's electoral roll.

🗓️
Phase 3

Early & Mail-In Voting

Many jurisdictions offer early in-person voting or allow mail-in ballots to be submitted weeks before the official Election Day.

🗳️
Phase 4 — Main Event

Election Day

The official day for in-person voting. Polling stations open at set hours. Bring your ID and head to your designated location.

📊
Phase 5

Vote Counting

Ballots are tallied securely and transparently under observation. Results begin emerging on election night as precincts report in.

✅
Phase 6

Certification & Inauguration

Official results are certified by electoral authorities over the following days or weeks. Winners are then sworn into office.
